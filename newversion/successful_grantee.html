Successful Grantees

Congratulations on receiving a White Levy Grant! Below you will find information regarding your contract, payment options, and how to successfully receive funding for up to three years.

The Contract

Please email the White Levy Program (info@whitelevy.org) to notify us of your current mailing address. Once we have received your mailing address, we will mail you a contract. Upon its arrival please read thoroughly and sign the contract, and send it back to:

The Shelby White & Leon Levy Program for Archaeological Publications
Harvard University, The Semitic Museum
6 Divinity Avenue, Cambridge, MA 02138

Your grant will only be paid out when we have received your original signed contract (no copies, scans, etc.). Expect this year's contract to arrive at the end of April or beginning of May, 2013.

Additional Information for our Website

Please also email us the title of your project, your university or other affiliation, and your full name as you would like it to be listed on our website.

 

Payment Options

There are two payment options: Personal Payment or Institutional Payment. Within these two options grantees may choose to receive their payment as a bank wire or a cheque. Please read the details below for the payment option of your choice. All money will be dispersed in June (assuming we have received your signed contract). All of the forms listed here will be mailed to you, but we encourage you to fill them out digitally using the PDFs below and email them to us.

A. PERSONAL PAYMENT
Accept the grant money personally and be responsible for any accrued taxes. United States citizens are reminded to file both federal and state income tax forms. If non-United States citizens select this option they are reminded to file appropriate tax forms in their country. Non-United States citizens who conduct their research within the United States will be subject to a 30% or more withholding tax in addition to whatever taxes their own government requires. Grantees are allowed to use grant funds to pay taxes on the awarded money.

If you would like to receive your grant money as a personal payment, please fill out ONE form from EACH column:

B. INSTITUTIONAL PAYMENT
Arrange an institutional account (see below for a definition of "institution") for the purpose of using all of the money for the project. If the grantee selects this option, the grantee is fully responsible for setting up the account with an institution or foundation, etc. The grantee must provide the White-Levy Program office with all account numbers, addresses and the name in which the check is to be issued. The grantee or institution is responsible for confirming the arrival of the check by contacting the White-Levy Program office.

The Program defines "institution" as any academic organization, such as a university or college, etc., or a private foundation which will administer the grantee's White Levy award. Personal bank accounts or the like are not considered an institutional account.

If you would like to receive your grant money as an Institutional Payment, please have your designated institution fill out the Vendor Registration form and EITHER a bank wire or a cheque request.

Second Year Funding

In order to make sure that you and your team have funds to support your work during the summer months, a three-month "bridging grant" will be issued on June 15th (to cover July, August, and September). This bridging grant will comprise the first quarter of your 2nd year of funding. In order to receive the remainder of your funding, you must submit an annual report by August 1st. The Board will review the report and approve or deny it at their September meeting. If approved, a second check will then be issued on October 1, disbursing the remaining 9 months of the grant.

 

Third Year Funding

An annual report on your project is due in January along with updated publication information. You will receive funding after a successful review of your status report.

Continued Reporting

You are required to submit an annual report every year until your project is published.
